What is SERE?

In Simple Terms

SERE (Sustainable Exergy Risk Engine) is a smart decision-making tool that helps energy companies avoid costly mistakes when buying and selling electricity.

Think of it like a weather forecast for your business:
- Weather forecast tells you if it will rain -> You decide to bring an umbrella
- SERE tells you if prices might spike or supply might fail -> You decide to buy backup power or adjust contracts

What Does It Do?

SERE helps you answer three critical questions:

  1. What could go wrong? (Risk Analysis)

    • Will we have enough power tomorrow?
    • Could prices suddenly spike?
    • What's our worst-case scenario?

  2. How much could it cost us? (Financial Impact)

    • If things go wrong, how much money are we risking?
    • What's the maximum loss we might face?

  3. What should we do about it? (Action Recommendations)

    • Should we buy more power now?
    • Should we use our backup storage?
    • Should we wait and do nothing?

Why Is This Important?

Real-world scenario:
- Your company promised to deliver 100 MW of power tomorrow
- Your solar panels might only produce 20 MW (cloudy day expected)
- You need to buy the remaining 80 MW from the market
- But what if the price suddenly jumps to €300/MWh?
- SERE calculates this risk and tells you the best action to take TODAY

Who Should Use This Manual?

This manual is for YOU if you are:

  • Forecast Team Member: You input predictions about tomorrow's power supply and demand
  • Management/Trader: You make decisions about buying, selling, or adjusting positions
  • Risk Manager: You monitor and control financial exposure
  • Energy Analyst: You analyze market conditions and make recommendations
  • Complete Beginner: You have ZERO experience with energy trading or risk systems

No Prior Knowledge Required

You do NOT need to know:
- Programming or coding
- Advanced statistics or mathematics
- Energy trading jargon
- Risk management theory

We'll explain everything in plain language!

Understanding the Basics

The Power Balance Equation

Every hour, your company must balance:

SUPPLY = DEMAND

Where:
Supply = Solar (PV) + Wind + Purchased Power + Storage
Demand = Customer Needs + Contracts

If Supply < Demand: You face a SHORTAGE -> Must buy expensive emergency power
If Supply > Demand: You face a SURPLUS -> Must sell at low prices or waste power

SERE's job: Predict these imbalances BEFORE they happen and recommend actions.

Key Concepts for Beginners

1. Probabilistic Forecast (P10, P50, P90)

Imagine tomorrow's solar output could be anywhere between 10 MW and 30 MW. We express this as:

  • P10 = 12 MW: "10% chance it will be THIS LOW or lower" (Worst case)
  • P50 = 20 MW: "50% chance it will be around THIS" (Most likely)
  • P90 = 28 MW: "90% chance it will be THIS HIGH or lower" (Best case)

Simple analogy:
- P10 = "Very cloudy day"
- P50 = "Normal day"
- P90 = "Very sunny day"

2. Risk Metrics

  • Expected Loss: Average amount you might lose (like average rainfall)
  • VaR (Value at Risk): "95% of the time, losses won't exceed THIS amount"
  • CVaR (Conditional VaR): "If things go really bad (worst 5% of cases), expect to lose THIS much"

Example:
- Expected Loss = €500 -> "On average, you'll lose €500"
- VaR (95%) = €2,000 -> "95% of the time, losses stay under €2,000"
- CVaR (95%) = €3,500 -> "In worst-case scenarios (5% of times), you lose €3,500 on average"

3. Risk States

SERE classifies every situation into four levels:

Risk State Meaning Traffic Light What It Means
HOLD Very safe Green Do nothing - situation is stable
WATCH Monitor closely Yellow Be alert - potential risk building
ACTION Take action now Orange Act now to avoid losses
CRITICAL Emergency Red Immediate action required!

4. Mitigation Actions

These are things you can DO to reduce risk:

Action Simple Explanation When to Use
Intraday Purchase Buy power from the market NOW When you expect shortage
Intraday Sale Sell excess power NOW When you have too much
Demand Flexibility Ask customers to use less power During expensive hours
Storage Charging Put power into batteries When prices are low
Storage Discharging Use battery power When prices are high
Hedge Adjustment Change your forward contracts To lock in prices
Curtailment Shut down non-critical loads In extreme emergencies
Emergency Response Activate backup plans Critical situations only

For Forecast Team Users

Your Role

You are the eyes of the system. Your job is to predict what will happen tomorrow so Management can make smart decisions today.

Step-by-Step: Submitting a Forecast

Step 1: Log In

  1. Open SERE in your browser
  2. Click " Forecast Team" button
  3. You'll see the main forecast input screen

Step 2: Enter Demand Forecast

What is this? How much power your customers will need tomorrow.

  1. Look at the "Demand Forecast (MW)" section

  2. Enter three numbers:

    • P10 (Pessimistic): Lowest demand you expect

    • Example: 85 MW -> "Customers will use at least this much"

    • P50 (Expected): Most likely demand

    • Example: 100 MW -> "Probably around this"

    • P90 (Optimistic): Highest demand you expect

    • Example: 115 MW -> "Could go this high"

How to estimate:
- Check historical data for similar days (same weekday, season, weather)
- Look at weather forecasts (hot/cold days = higher demand)
- Consider special events (holidays, festivals)

Step 3: Enter PV (Solar) Generation Forecast

What is this? How much power your solar panels will produce tomorrow.

  1. Find "PV Generation Forecast (MW)" section

  2. Enter three numbers:

    • P10: Worst case (very cloudy)

    • Example: 12 MW

    • P50: Most likely (normal weather)

    • Example: 20 MW

    • P90: Best case (very sunny)

    • Example: 28 MW

Tips:
- Check weather forecasts (cloud cover, temperature)
- Consider season (winter = less sun, summer = more sun)
- Account for panel efficiency and maintenance

Step 4: Enter Wind Generation Forecast

What is this? How much power your wind turbines will produce tomorrow.

  1. Find "Wind Generation Forecast (MW)" section

  2. Enter three numbers:

    • P10: Low wind scenario

    • Example: 18 MW

    • P50: Normal wind

    • Example: 30 MW

    • P90: High wind scenario

    • Example: 42 MW

Tips:
- Check wind speed forecasts from meteorology services
- Higher wind speed = more power (but too high = turbines shut down!)
- Consider seasonal patterns

Step 5: Enter Market Prices

What is this? How much it costs to buy or sell power in different markets.

  1. Day-Ahead Price (€/MWh): Tomorrow's fixed price

    • Example: 100€/MWh
    • Where to find: Check your day-ahead market results

  2. Intraday Bid (€/MWh): Price to BUY power today

    • Example: 150€/MWh
    • Usually HIGHER than day-ahead (you pay premium for last-minute buying)

  3. Intraday Ask (€/MWh): Price to SELL power today

    • Example: 140€/MWh
    • Usually LOWER than bid (you get less for last-minute selling)

  4. ReBAP+ (Shortage Penalty) (€/MWh): Fine if you deliver too little power

    • Example: 300€/MWh
    • This is EXPENSIVE! Avoid shortages!

  5. ReBAP- (Surplus Penalty) (€/MWh): Penalty if you deliver too much power

    • Example: -50€/MWh
    • Negative number = you pay or lose money

Step 6: Enter Current Hedge Position

What is this? How much power you've already committed to deliver through contracts.

  • Hedge Position (MW): Your current obligation
    • Example: 50 MW -> "You promised to deliver 50 MW"

Step 7: Set Confidence Level

What is this? How confident are you in your forecast?

  • Slide the bar from 0% to 100%
  • 80% = "Pretty confident, normal conditions"
  • 60% = "Uncertain, weather is unpredictable"
  • 95% = "Very confident, stable conditions"

Step 8: Add Notes (Optional)

Write any important context:
- "Storm expected in the afternoon"
- "Holiday weekend - lower demand expected"
- "Solar panel maintenance scheduled"

Step 9: Validate Your Forecast

  1. Click the " VALIDATE FORECAST" button (center of screen)
  2. SERE will check your data for errors:

* If validation PASSES:*
- You'll see green checkmark: " Validation Passed - Data looks good!"
- A new button appears: " SUBMIT FORECAST"

* If validation FAILS:*
- You'll see red error messages explaining what's wrong
- Common errors:
- "P10 ≤ P50 ≤ P90 ordering violated" -> Your numbers are in wrong order
- "Demand uncertainty too high" -> Difference between P10 and P90 is too large
- "Generation cannot be negative" -> You entered a negative number for solar/wind

  1. Fix any errors and click Validate again

Step 10: Submit to Management

  1. After successful validation, click " SUBMIT FORECAST"
  2. You'll see:
    • Success message
    • Balloons animation
    • Confirmation that Management can now see your forecast

Congratulations! Your forecast is submitted. Management can now use it to make decisions.

Step 11: Switch to Management View (Optional)

If you want to see what Management sees:

  1. Click " Switch Role" button (top right)
  2. Select " Management"
  3. You'll see your submitted forecast and can execute the decision engine

For Management Users

Your Role

You are the decision maker. Based on the Forecast Team's predictions, you decide what actions to take to minimize risk and maximize profit.

Step-by-Step: Making Decisions

Step 1: Log In

  1. Open SERE in your browser
  2. Click " Management" button
  3. You'll see the main risk control dashboard

Step 2: Check If Forecast Is Available

*Scenario A: Forecast Available *
- You'll see four tabs: " Decision Engine", " Risk Dashboard", " Loss Distribution", " Settings"
- Proceed to Step 3

Scenario B: No Forecast Available *
- You'll see: " No validated forecast available from Forecast Team"
- *Option 1: Wait for Forecast Team to submit data
- Option 2: Click " Load Demo Data" button to test with sample data

Step 3: Review Forecast Summary

At the top of the screen, you'll see four key metrics:

Metric What It Means
Demand (P50) Expected customer demand (middle estimate)
PV Generation (P50) Expected solar output
Wind Generation (P50) Expected wind output
Net Position Your balance (negative = shortage, positive = surplus)

Example:
- Demand: 100 MW ±15
- PV: 20 MW ±8
- Wind: 30 MW ±12
- Net Position: -30 MW -> "You're short by 30 MW!"

Step 4: Execute the Decision Engine

  1. Click the " EXECUTE DECISION ENGINE" button (big blue button)
  2. Wait 3-10 seconds while SERE analyzes 10,000 possible scenarios
  3. Results will appear below

Step 5: Understand the Decision Output

After execution, you'll see several sections:

A) Risk State Card (Top)

This is your traffic light:

 RISK STATE: HOLD
 "No immediate action needed - situation is manageable"

Or:

 RISK STATE: CRITICAL
 "Immediate action required to prevent significant losses"

What to do for each state:

  • * HOLD*: Relax. Monitor but don't act. Risk is low.
  • * WATCH*: Be alert. Prepare to act if conditions worsen.
  • * ACTION*: Act now. Follow recommended mitigation actions.
  • * CRITICAL*: Emergency! Execute recommendations immediately.
B) Risk Metrics

Three key numbers that quantify your risk:

Expected Loss: €713.28
VaR (95%): €2,630.22
CVaR (95%): €3,505.78

What do these mean?

  • Expected Loss (€713):

    • "On average, this situation will cost us €713"
    • This is the most likely outcome

  • VaR (95%) (€2,630):

    • "95 times out of 100, our losses won't exceed €2,630"
    • This is your normal worst case
    • Budget for this amount in normal risk management

  • CVaR (95%) (€3,505):

    • "In the worst 5% of scenarios, we lose €3,505 on average"
    • This is your extreme worst case (tail risk)
    • This is what keeps you up at night!

Risk Budget Check:
- If CVaR > Your Risk Limit -> MUST ACT!
- If CVaR < Your Risk Limit -> You can handle this

C) Recommended Actions

SERE will list 1-5 specific actions ranked by effectiveness:

Recommended Actions:
1. Intraday Purchase: 32.5 MW @ €150/MWh
 Cost: €4,875
 CVaR Reduction: €2,314 (saves more than it costs!)
 Net Benefit: +€2,314

How to read this:

  • Action: What to do -> "Buy 32.5 MW in intraday market"
  • Cost: How much it costs -> €4,875
  • CVaR Reduction: How much risk it eliminates -> €2,314
  • Net Benefit: Total value -> Positive = Good action!

Decision Rules:

DO execute if:
- Net Benefit is POSITIVE (saves more than it costs)
- CVaR Reduction is LARGE (significantly lowers risk)
- Risk State is ACTION or CRITICAL

DON'T execute if:
- Net Benefit is NEGATIVE (costs more than it saves)
- CVaR Reduction is SMALL (doesn't help much)
- Risk State is HOLD (no action needed)

D) Confidence Factors

These tell you how reliable the decision is:

Convergence: 98.5% (Good - simulation is stable)
Sensitivity: Medium (Moderate price sensitivity)
Forecast Quality: 80% (Good confidence)

Interpretation:

  • Convergence > 95%: Trust the numbers
  • Convergence < 90%: Run simulation again
  • Sensitivity = High: Small price changes = big impact
  • Sensitivity = Low: Prices matter less
  • Forecast Quality: How confident Forecast Team was

Step 6: Execute Recommended Actions

If you agree with SERE's recommendations:

  1. Open your trading platform (separate system)
  2. Execute the recommended trades:
    • Example: Buy 32.5 MW in intraday market at €150/MWh
  3. Document the trade in your systems
  4. (Optional) Come back to SERE and log that you executed it

If you disagree or want to wait:

  • SERE is a recommendation tool, not an auto-trader
  • You have final decision authority
  • Document why you didn't follow the recommendation (for audit purposes)

Step 7: Monitor Risk Dashboard

Click the " Risk Dashboard" tab to see:

  1. Risk Metrics Over Time: Chart showing how CVaR evolved
  2. Exposure by Component: Which factors contribute most to risk
  3. Scenario Distribution: Histogram of 10,000 simulated outcomes

Use this to:
- Understand where your risk comes from
- Identify patterns (e.g., "wind uncertainty is killing us")
- Communicate to executives

Step 8: Analyze Loss Distribution

Click the " Loss Distribution" tab to see:

  • Histogram: Visual distribution of possible losses
  • VaR and CVaR lines: Where the thresholds are
  • Tail risk: How bad the worst cases can get

This helps you:
- Visualize the full range of outcomes
- See if risk is symmetric or skewed
- Understand probability of extreme events

Real-World Example Walkthrough

Let's walk through a complete example:

The Situation

Forecast Team submitted:
- Tomorrow's demand: 100 MW (expected)
- Solar will produce: 20 MW (expected)
- Wind will produce: 30 MW (expected)

SERE's Analysis

After running 10,000 scenarios:

 RISK STATE: ACTION
Expected Loss: €1,250
VaR (95%): €4,200
CVaR (95%): €6,800

Translation:
- Orange "ACTION" state = You need to act!
- On average, this shortage will cost €1,250
- 95% of the time, losses stay under €4,200
- In worst case (5%), you lose €6,800

SERE's Recommendation

1. Intraday Purchase: 35 MW @ €150/MWh
 Cost: €5,250
 CVaR Reduction: €4,500
 Net Benefit: -€750 (costs more but necessary!)

Your Decision

Option A: Follow the recommendation
- You buy 35 MW now at €150/MWh
- Cost: €5,250
- But this prevents potential €6,800 loss
- Net: You pay €5,250 to avoid €6,800 risk
- Effective savings: €1,550 + you sleep better!

Option B: Ignore and gamble
- Don't buy now, wait and see
- If you're lucky: Wind/solar overperform, you're fine
- If unlucky: You pay ReBAP+ penalty (€300/MWh) on shortage
- Potential loss: Up to €6,800
- High risk!

Best practice: Follow ACTION and CRITICAL recommendations. You can skip WATCH and HOLD actions.

Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

Q: Do I need to be a programmer or mathematician to use SERE?
A: No! SERE is designed for business users. If you can use Excel, you can use SERE.

Q: How long does it take to learn SERE?
A: Most users are productive within 1-2 hours. Mastery takes 1-2 weeks of daily use.

Q: Can I break something or cause real trades?
A: No! SERE is a decision support tool. It recommends actions but never executes trades automatically.

Q: How accurate is SERE?
A: SERE is as accurate as your input forecasts. Garbage in = garbage out. Good forecasts = good decisions.

Forecast Team Questions

Q: What if I don't have P10/P90 forecasts?
A: Use simple rules:
- P10 = P50 - 15%
- P90 = P50 + 15%
- Adjust based on uncertainty (more uncertain = wider range)

Q: How do I estimate market prices?
A: Use recent market data:
- Day-Ahead: Check official exchange results
- Intraday: Use recent trades ±10€
- ReBAP: Use regulatory penalty rates

Q: What if my forecast is wrong?
A: That's normal! No forecast is perfect. SERE accounts for uncertainty. Just submit your best estimate.

Q: Can I update my forecast after submission?
A: Yes! Just submit a new forecast. It will overwrite the previous one.

Q: How often should I submit forecasts?
A: Typically once per day (for next day). More often if conditions change dramatically.

Management Questions

Q: Should I always follow SERE's recommendations?
A: Follow ACTION and CRITICAL recommendations. WATCH and HOLD are informational.

Q: What if recommended action costs more than CVaR reduction?
A: Sometimes you pay for "insurance." If risk is unacceptable, pay for mitigation even if net benefit is negative.

Q: Can I override SERE's decision?
A: Yes! You're the final decision maker. Document why you overrode (for compliance).

Q: What does "Manual Override Required" mean?
A: Situation is too complex or risky for automated recommendation. Human judgment needed.

Q: How do I know if the decision is reliable?
A: Check "Convergence" percentage:
- >95% = Reliable
- 90-95% = Use caution
- <90% = Don't trust, run again

Q: Why does CVaR matter more than Expected Loss?
A: Expected Loss = average. CVaR = extreme scenarios. You budget for average but protect against extremes!

Technical Questions

Q: Why 10,000 scenarios?
A: More scenarios = more accurate but slower. 10,000 is the sweet spot for accuracy vs. speed.

Q: What is Monte Carlo simulation?
A: Fancy term for "try thousands of different possibilities and see what happens each time."

Q: Do I need to understand the math?
A: No! You just need to understand the outputs (CVaR, recommendations, risk state).

Q: What if simulation takes too long?
A: Reduce scenarios to 5,000 in Settings tab. Slightly less accurate but faster.

Q: Why are there sometimes no recommended actions?
A: Risk is already low (HOLD state). No action needed = best action is to do nothing!

Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

Issue 1: Can't Submit Forecast

Symptom: Validation fails with error messages

Solutions:
1. Check P10 ≤ P50 ≤ P90 ordering (numbers must increase)
2. Ensure all generation values are positive (no negatives)
3. Check that uncertainty isn't too large (P90 - P10 shouldn't be huge)
4. Make sure all required fields are filled

Issue 2: No Submit Button After Validation

Symptom: Clicked Validate, but no Submit button appears

Solutions:
1. Refresh the page (press F5)
2. Check if validation actually passed (green checkmark?)
3. Look below the validation message - button might be there
4. Try validating again

Issue 3: Forecast Not Appearing in Management View

Symptom: Management sees "No forecast available" after Forecast Team submitted

Solutions:
1. Use " Switch Role" button instead of "Logout"
- Logout clears data!
- Switch Role keeps data!
2. Make sure you clicked "Submit Forecast" (not just Validate)
3. Refresh the browser page
4. Check you're in the same browser tab/session

Issue 4: Decision Engine Runs But Shows No Actions

Symptom: Execution completes but "No actions recommended"

Solutions:
- This is NORMAL for HOLD state!
- If risk is low, no action is needed
- Check Risk State - if it says HOLD, you're good
- Look at CVaR - if it's below your risk limit, no action needed

Issue 5: CVaR Seems Too High

Symptom: CVaR is €50,000+ and seems unrealistic

Solutions:
1. Check your price inputs (did you accidentally enter 3000 instead of 300?)
2. Check your demand/generation forecasts (did you enter GW instead of MW?)
3. Review P10/P90 spread (too wide = inflated risk)
4. Adjust correlations in Advanced settings

Issue 6: Convergence Below 90%

Symptom: "Convergence: 85% Low reliability"

Solutions:
1. Increase scenarios to 20,000 in Settings tab
2. Wait a bit longer (give it 20-30 seconds)
3. Check if inputs are extreme (very large numbers can cause instability)
4. If still low, contact system administrator

Issue 7: Browser Freezes During Execution

Symptom: Screen freezes when clicking "Execute"

Solutions:
1. Wait! Simulation takes 5-15 seconds - it's working
2. Look for spinning wheel or "Running..." message
3. Don't click multiple times (patience!)
4. If frozen for >60 seconds, refresh page and try again

Issue 8: Can't See Charts or Visualizations

Symptom: Empty boxes where charts should be

Solutions:
1. Scroll down - charts might be below the fold
2. Refresh page (F5)
3. Check internet connection (charts need Plotly library)
4. Try a different browser (Chrome recommended)
5. Disable ad blockers (they sometimes block chart libraries)

Issue 9: Lost My Submitted Forecast

Symptom: Data disappeared after switching roles

Solutions:
- Use "Switch Role" not "Logout"
- Logout clears everything (by design)
- Prevention: Always use Switch Role to keep data in session

Issue 10: Demo Data Button Not Working

Symptom: Click "Load Demo Data" but nothing happens

Solutions:
1. Click once and wait (don't click multiple times)
2. Check if data appeared in the summary cards above
3. Refresh page and try again
4. If still not working, contact support

Appendix: Mathematical Details (Optional)

This section is for advanced users who want to understand the underlying math.

Monte Carlo Simulation

SERE generates 10,000+ random scenarios using:

Demand_scenario = Normal(μ=P50, σ derived from P10-P90)
PV_scenario = Normal(μ=P50, σ derived from P10-P90)
Wind_scenario = Normal(μ=P50, σ derived from P10-P90)

With correlation matrix:
 Demand PV Wind
Demand 1.0 ρ_d,pv ρ_d,w
PV ρ_d,pv 1.0 ρ_pv,w
Wind ρ_d,w ρ_pv,w 1.0

CVaR Calculation

CVaR(95%) = E[Loss | Loss > VaR(95%)]
 = Mean of worst 5% of scenarios

Mitigation Optimization

For each action type, SERE solves:

Minimize: CVaR_post_mitigation + λ × Cost
Subject to: Operational constraints
 Market limits
 Physical feasibility

Risk State Thresholds

HOLD: CVaR < Threshold_1
WATCH: Threshold_1 ≤ CVaR < Threshold_2
ACTION: Threshold_2 ≤ CVaR < Threshold_3
CRITICAL: CVaR ≥ Threshold_3
